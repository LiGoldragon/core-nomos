# Non-idealities — core-nomos

Recorded debt with a future fix target. Ordinary rules live in `AGENTS.md`; the
ideal shape lives in `ARCHITECTURE.md`. Each entry names the symptom, the current
workaround, and the proper fix or the design question the psyche must settle.

## The class-D `TraceEvent` tuple-struct declaration is not byte-exact-projectable

- **Symptom:** the `spirit_generated.rs` golden declares
  `pub struct TraceEvent(pub ObjectName);` — a tuple newtype with a **`pub` field**.
  The frozen `core-logos` `Newtype` (`{ visibility, attributes, name, wrapped }`)
  models no tuple-field visibility, so projecting it yields
  `pub struct TraceEvent(ObjectName);` (no inner `pub`), which is not byte-exact.
  `textual-rust`'s reader confirms the gap: it rejects the block as
  "a tuple newtype with a visibility-qualified field is not one of the modeled item
  kinds".
- **Workaround:** the class-D `TraceSupport` generator emits the `TraceEvent`
  *impl* (byte-exact) but **not** the `TraceEvent` struct declaration. The enriched
  byte-exact test (`tests/enriched.rs`) covers the emitted items and documents the
  omission; it makes no byte-exact claim for the struct declaration.
- **Proper fix:** a `core-logos` kernel change — model tuple-field visibility on
  `Newtype` (a `pub`-field tuple struct), and teach `textual-rust` to read/project
  it. `core-logos` is foundation for this slice (pinned at `b6198df2`), so the fix is
  out of scope here and rides a later kernel-extension cascade. Until then the
  `TraceEvent` struct declaration is generated by the frozen schema-rust path, not by
  core-nomos.
